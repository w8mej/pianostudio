The Instructor

About Lisa Menerick

A celebrated educator, performer, and former Mayor of Ferrysburg — Lisa Menerick brings a lifetime of musical passion and community dedication to every lesson.

Originally from East Grand Rapids — where she grew up across the street from President Gerald R. Ford — Lisa has been a fixture in the West Michigan music scene for decades. A theater education major at Michigan State University, she also holds degrees from Sullins College (Bristol, VA), Wayne State University, and Grand Valley State University.

Lisa spent nearly 50 years as an elementary educator, most recently with Muskegon Public Schools, while simultaneously teaching private piano and voice for over 40 years. Her performing career has taken her from intimate local nursing homes to singing the national anthem at Loutit District Library's naturalization ceremony, performing at the annual Ferrysburg Carol Sing, and singing for the Professional Bull Riders as a licensed rodeo consultant.

Lisa has been a proud member of the National Guild of Piano Teachers since 1979 and is a member of the St. Cecelia Music Society. She performs and lectures professionally, offering private and group piano and voice lessons to students from age 4 through adult — from studios in Grand Rapids and along the Lakeshore.
